A peptide is a short chain of amino acids (typically 2 to 50 amino acids) linked by peptide bonds. Peptides are smaller than proteins and play critical roles in biological signaling, acting as hormones, neurotransmitters, and growth factors. Research peptides are synthetic versions of naturally occurring peptides, designed to study specific biological pathways.
