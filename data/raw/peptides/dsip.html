Pomfrett CJ, Dolling S, Anders NR et al. · Eur J Anaesthesiol (2009) [](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19142086/) RCT n=24 DSIP paradoxically reduced anaesthetic depth and delta rhythm while increasing heart rate during isoflurane anaesthesia. #### Effects of delta sleep-inducing peptide on sleep of chronic insomniac patients. A double-blind study. Bes F, Hofman W, Schuur J et al. · Neuropsychobiology (1992) [](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1299794/) RCT n=16 5 nights DSIP showed weak effects on sleep efficiency and latency; unlikely to provide major therapeutic benefit for chronic insomnia. #### Delta-sleep-inducing peptide does not affect CRH and meal-induced ACTH and cortisol secretion. Späth-Schwalbe E, Schäfer A, Uthgenannt D et al. · Psychoneuroendocrinology (1995) [](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/7777652/) RCT n=15 DSIP did not affect ACTH and cortisol secretion in response to CRH or meals. #### Effects of delta-sleep-inducing peptide on 24-hour sleep-wake behaviour in severe chronic insomnia. Schneider-Helmert D · Eur Neurol (1987) [](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3622582/) RCT n=14 7 nights DSIP improved night sleep efficiency and daytime alertness in chronic insomniacs. #### Study of delta sleep-inducing peptide efficacy in improving sleep on short-term administration to chronic insomniacs. Monti JM, Debellis J, Alterwain P et al. · Int J Clin Pharmacol Res (1987) [](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3583493/) RCT 4 nights DSIP increased NREM and stage 2 sleep but differences from placebo lacked clinical significance.
